Exercise 1: Making Change

You need to make change for 247 cents. How many quarters, dimes, nickels, and pennies?

Answer:

247 / 25 = 9 quarters (use 225 cents)

247 % 25 = 22 cents remaining

22 / 10 = 2 dimes (use 20 cents)

22 % 10 = 2 cents remaining

2 / 5 = 0 nickels

2 % 5 = 2 cents remaining

2 pennies

Result: 9 quarters, 2 dimes, 0 nickels, 2 pennies

Exercise 2: Time Conversion

Convert 5000 seconds into hours, minutes, and seconds.

Answer:

5000 / 3600 = 1 hour (use 3600 seconds)

5000 % 3600 = 1400 seconds remaining

1400 / 60 = 23 minutes (use 1380 seconds)

1400 % 60 = 20 seconds remaining

Result: 1 hour, 23 minutes, 20 seconds

Exercise 3: Distance Conversion

Convert 100 inches to feet and inches. (12 inches = 1 foot)

Answer:

100 / 12 = 8 feet (use 96 inches)

100 % 12 = 4 inches remaining

Result: 8 feet, 4 inches

🏆 Challenge Problem

Convert 10,000 seconds into days, hours, minutes, and seconds.

Hint: 1 day = 86,400 seconds, 1 hour = 3,600 seconds, 1 minute = 60 seconds

Solution:

10000 / 86400 = 0 days

10000 % 86400 = 10000 seconds remaining

10000 / 3600 = 2 hours (use 7200 seconds)

10000 % 3600 = 2800 seconds remaining

2800 / 60 = 46 minutes (use 2760 seconds)

2800 % 60 = 40 seconds remaining

Result: 0 days, 2 hours, 46 minutes, 40 seconds

📚 Key Takeaways

  • / operator gives you the count (how many complete groups)
  • % operator gives you the remainder (what's left over)
  • Greedy algorithms process largest units first for optimal results
  • Always use a "remaining" variable to track the remainder between steps
  • The pattern: extract unit → calculate remainder → move to next smaller unit

Keep practicing! The more you use these operators, the more natural they'll become.
